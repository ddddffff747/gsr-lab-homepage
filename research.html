01

Earthquake–Soil Interaction

We investigate how soils respond to earthquake ground motions, with particular emphasis on amplification, attenuation, and nonlinear deformation of soil deposits.

Our research focuses on understanding seismic wave propagation through different soil conditions and stratigraphic profiles, aiming to characterize and predict site-specific seismic behavior across regions.

These studies provide a fundamental basis for interpreting earthquake-induced ground motions and improving seismic design practices.

02

Seismic Ground Failure & Hazard Assessment

This research area focuses on earthquake-induced ground failures such as liquefaction, ground deformation, and slope instability.

Using observations from real earthquake events together with analytical and numerical approaches, we evaluate the mechanisms, likelihood, and severity of seismic ground failures.

The ultimate goal is to quantitatively assess earthquake-related ground hazards and support risk-informed mitigation and design strategies.

03

Seismic Behavior of Geotechnical & Underground Structures

We study the seismic response of geotechnical and underground structures, including tunnels, underground transit systems, retaining walls, and foundation systems.

By explicitly considering soil–structure interaction, our research aims to identify damage mechanisms, deformation characteristics, and seismic vulnerability of these systems.

The outcomes contribute to performance-based seismic evaluation and resilient design of underground and geotechnical infrastructure.

Tunnels SSI Analysis Infrastructure
04

Numerical Simulation & Predictive Modeling

This research area develops advanced numerical simulation frameworks to model the complex behavior of soils and structures under seismic loading.

Through multi-dimensional and nonlinear analyses, we simulate realistic earthquake scenarios and quantify seismic response and risk.

Predictive models are developed to efficiently evaluate seismic performance of geotechnical systems while accounting for material nonlinearity and uncertainty.

FEM Analysis Nonlinear Modeling Risk Assessment
05

Artificial Intelligence & Machine Learning

Artificial intelligence and machine learning techniques are applied using data obtained from field measurements and rigorously conducted site response and numerical analyses.

By integrating physics-based understanding with data-driven approaches, we enhance the interpretation and prediction of seismic behavior of soils and underground structures.

This research aims to complement conventional analytical methods and improve the efficiency and reliability of seismic assessment of complex geotechnical systems.
